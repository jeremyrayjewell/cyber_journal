## Overview  

Section 7 introduces Group Policy and covers how GPOs are created, linked, and applied within a domain.

---

## Module 7.1: Overview of Group Policies  

**Learning Objectives:**  
- Understand what Group Policy is and how it is used in Windows domains  
- Identify how Group Policy Objects (GPOs) are applied to users and computers  

**Key Topics:**  
- Purpose of Group Policy in centralized configuration management  
- Group Policy Objects (GPOs) and their scope  
- How GPOs are linked to sites, domains, and OUs  
- Basic order of processing and inheritance  
- Common use cases for Group Policy in enterprise environments  

---

## Module 7.2: Creating, Editing, and Linking GPOs  

**Learning Objectives:**  
- Create and edit Group Policy Objects  
- Link GPOs to OUs, domains, or sites to apply policies  

**Key Topics:**  
- Creating new GPOs  
- Editing policy settings with Group Policy Management  
- Linking GPOs to target scopes  
- Understanding GPO precedence and inheritance  
- Testing and validating policy application  

---

## Module 7.3: Group Policy Precedence  

**Learning Objectives:**  
- Understand how Group Policy precedence determines which settings apply  
- Resolve conflicts between multiple GPOs  

**Key Topics:**  
- GPO processing order (LSDOU: Local, Site, Domain, OU)  
- Inheritance and enforcement behavior  
- Blocking inheritance  
- How conflicting GPO settings are resolved  
- Common causes of unexpected policy application  

---

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

- GPOs enable centralized configuration of users and computers.  
- Policy precedence affects which settings ultimately apply.  
- Proper scoping and testing prevent unintended policy impact.
